Inside Wombs (2021)

My personal experiences related to the mother and child theme inspired these works. I have experienced accompanying my mother during the loss of her twenty-week unborn child during my high school period, being present for my mother’s childbirth in the operating room, as well as being the one who cut the umbilical cord for my younger sister. In these works, I imagine myself inside a womb-like interior, floating in a watery space surrounded by veins, blood, and amniotic fluid. I want to reexperience the bonding with my mother and being inside my mother’s body and harbour. The womb-like interior spaces also arouse ambiguous feelings of comfort and fear. I also explore the contradiction between mother and foetus. Investigating how pregnant women feel disconnected from their bodies and the development of a new relationship with an unfamiliar entity
